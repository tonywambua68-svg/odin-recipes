ugali-sukuma

A traditional Kenyan meal of firm maize meal served with nutritious sautéed collard greens:

Ugali and Sukuma Wiki is a beloved Kenyan dish made by pairing soft, firm maize meal with freshly sautéed collard greens.

It is a staple in Kenyan households, often enjoyed with meat, fish, or vegetables, and is loved for its hearty texture, balanced flavors, and simplicity, making it perfect for everyday meals as well as family gatherings.

A traditional Kenyan meal of firm maize meal served with sautéed collard greens.

  • 2 cup maize flour(for ugali)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bunch sukuma wiki (collard greens), chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 spoonful cooking oil
  • salt to test
  • 2 tomatoes chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder or 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper (optional)

How to Cook Ugali and Sukuma Wiki;

  1. Prepare the ugali: Boil 4 cups of water in a pot
  2. Slowly add 2 cups of maize flour while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  3. Keep stirring until the mixture thickens and becomes firm.
  4. Shape the ugali into a round mound and set aside.
  5. Prepare the sukuma wiki: Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan.
  6. Add chopped onion and sauté until soft.
  7. Add the chopped sukuma wiki (collard greens) and stir.
  8. Season with salt, black pepper, and optional garlic or chilies.
  9. Cook until the greens are tender but still bright green.
  10. Serve: Place the sukuma wiki next to the ugali on a plate and enjoy
